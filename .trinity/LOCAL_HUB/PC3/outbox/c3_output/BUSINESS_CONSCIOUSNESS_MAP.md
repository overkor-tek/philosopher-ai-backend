## 3Ô∏è‚É£ SALES

### Unconscious Sales (0-40%)

**Approach:**
- Push products
- Maximize revenue per customer
- Close at any cost
- Overcome objections (manipulate)
- Qualify based on ability to pay only

**Characteristics:**
- High pressure tactics
- False urgency
- Objection handling scripts (manipulation)
- "Always be closing"
- Commission drives behavior

**Red Flags:**
- "How do we overcome their objections?"
- "What closing technique works?"
- "How do we get them to yes?"
- "They can afford it, so push harder"

**Results:**
- Short-term: High close rate on poor fits
- Long-term: Refunds, chargebacks, bad reviews
- Customer satisfaction: Low
- Referrals: None (they warn others away)
- Sustainability: High churn

### Conscious Sales (85%+)

**Approach:**
- Match solutions to needs
- Maximize value for customer
- Advise honestly (even if it means no sale)
- Address objections (clarify, not manipulate)
- Qualify based on fit, not just ability to pay

**Characteristics:**
- No pressure (collaborative)
- Real timing (when they're ready)
- Honest objection handling (may disqualify self)
- "Always be helping"
- Long-term relationship drives behavior

**Green Flags:**
- "Is this actually right for them?"
- "What's the best solution for their situation?" (might not be us)
- "Would we want to buy if in their shoes?" (Golden Rule)
- "How do we ensure they succeed?"

**Results:**
- Short-term: Lower close rate, but perfect fits only
- Long-term: Low refunds, great reviews, case studies
- Customer satisfaction: High
- Referrals: Many (they rave to others)
- Sustainability: Low churn, high LTV

### Transformation Path: Sales

| Level | Consciousness | Sales Approach |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| 0-20% | Asleep | High pressure, any close is good |
| 20-40% | Awakening | Reducing pressure, feeling guilty about tactics |
| 40-60% | Developing | Solution selling, some pressure still |
| 60-80% | Strong | Advisory selling, Golden Rule tested |
| 80-100% | Alteration | Trusted advisor, customers sell themselves |

**Key Tool:** Golden Rule - "Would I buy this, at this price, in this situation?"

**Example Scenario:**

**Prospect:** "I'm not sure I can afford this."

**Unconscious Response:** "That's why you NEED it! This will pay for itself! Can you afford NOT to?"

**Conscious Response:** "I understand. Let's look at your situation. If the ROI isn't clear and positive, you probably shouldn't buy. Here's how to calculate it for your case..."

Result: Sometimes they don't buy (but they trust you), sometimes they buy with conviction (and succeed).

- Fear-based motivation
- Information hoarding (knowledge = power)
- Decision centralization
- Ego-driven

- Mistakes punished
- Need-to-know information only
- All decisions through leader

**Red Flags:**
- "Because I said so"
- "Don't question me"
- "You don't need to know why"

**Results:**
- Short-term: Fast decisions (but often wrong)

- Align with truth
- Purpose-based motivation
- Information sharing (transparency = trust)
- Decision distribution
- Service-driven

**Characteristics:**
- "Here's why, decide together"
- Mistakes = learning opportunities
- Radical transparency (truth accessible)
- Push decisions down (autonomy)
- Leader fallible (admits wrong fast)

**Green Flags:**
- "What does truth/emergence say?" (not "what do I think?")
- "Let's examine my assumptions"
- "Here's full context, what do you think?"
- "This is your decision, I trust you"

**Results:**
- Short-term: Slower decisions (but higher quality)
- Long-term: Team autonomous, innovative, capable
- Team capability: Highly developed (constant practice)
- Turnover: Low (people love conscious leaders)
